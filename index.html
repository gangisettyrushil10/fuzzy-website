"The man who has a conscience suffers whilst acknowledging his sin. That is his punishment — as well as the prison." — Crime and Punishment, Dostoevsky
✦ Fuzzy
Dostoevsky argues that guilt itself is the real sentence. Raskolnikov's psychological torment before any arrest shows this — external punishment merely externalises what the conscience already inflicts. It's a deeply Christian idea: confession and suffering are redemptive, not just punitive.
